What is Cyber Security?

Cyber Security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Why is Cyber Security Important?

  • Protection of Sensitive Data
  • Prevention of Cyber Attacks
  • Compliance
  • Economic Stability

Cyber Security Timeline

  • 1960s: Computer systems and networks
  • 1970s-1980s: ARPANET security focus
  • 1990s: Internet growth, firewalls, antivirus
  • 2000s: Strategic priority, encryption
  • 2010s-Present: Ransomware, phishing, APTs

Current Cyber Security Challenges

  • Rapidly evolving threat landscape
  • Sophisticated attack methods
  • Skills shortage in professionals
  • IoT adoption challenges
  • Remote work security

Cyber Security Best Practices

  • Defense in Depth: Multiple security layers
  • Risk Assessment: Regular threat evaluation
  • Security Awareness: Employee training
  • Incident Response: Prepared response plans
  • Continuous Monitoring: Ongoing surveillance

Emerging Technologies

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Machine Learning
  • Quantum Computing
  • Blockchain Security
  • Zero Trust Architecture

Emerging Threats

  • AI-powered attacks
  • Quantum computing threats
  • IoT vulnerabilities
  • Supply chain attacks
  • Deep fake technology

Key Takeaways

  • Cyber security is essential in digital world
  • Field evolved significantly over 60 years
  • Modern threats are sophisticated
  • Multi-layered approach necessary
  • Continuous learning crucial
